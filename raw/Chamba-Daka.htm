Other comments: Chamba-Daka has a complicated numeral system with special forms numbers 8 and 10. The linker 'bàà' is recorded as a verb meaning 'bud' or 'flower' (as a plant). The word 'mūm' means 'mouth, and is used symbolically for 'person', 'nōo' is a descriptive term meaning 'productive, spacious, or 'left (hand) and used referentially for 'enveloping parts of plants' and 'dense vegetation', 'bùm' means 'place, time', 'méém' means 'children'. Traditionally, counting would rarely have been done above '20'. The rest of the number system must be considered a fairly recent innovation, which may have been based on a model in another language; if so, this language has not yet been identified. There is another different system for counting money.
