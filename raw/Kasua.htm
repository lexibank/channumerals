Other comments: Kasua have a symmetrical body-part tally system with a cycle length of 35 and a mid-point at tip of nose. The suffix –ipe is “always used in counting except when using a combination of numbers, where the first numbers will not have it and the last one will”. It serves as a noun modifier: thus, kasolo taloipe translates as ‘ten dogs’ and sena talo pipe as ‘fifty men’.

Adaptation to English decimal system: 10. talo semetei (lit: ten one),

19. talo semetei epela:akotaipe (lit: 10, 1, 9),

20. talo elepei (lit: ten two), 25. talo elipe epela:piipe (lit: ten tow five),

100. sakopolo.
