Other comments: Mor has a quinary, vigesimal system of counting. The word ''naʔu'' is derived from ''one person''. The numerals go at least up to naʔu tàura = 200. After that, speakers become confused. In principle it should be possible to count up to naʔu tàura maʔa rímó maʔa áʔó maʔa tàura maʔa rímó maʔa áʔó = 399, however no speakers of Mor would be able to understand that. Mor is one of the few tonal Austronesian languages in Papua area, Indonesia.
