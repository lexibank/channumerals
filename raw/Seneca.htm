Other comments: Seneca has a decimal system. The phonetic symbolsː ë and ö are nasalized vowels. The colon indicates vowels length. The accent mark indicates a higher pitch. The 'sh' as in 20 and other tens are separated phonemes 's' and 'h'. 'dz' is a voices alveolar affricate and 'j (=dž) is a voiced palatal affricate. ' ʼ ' is a laryngeal stop.
