Other comments: The above data was from taken from Wadu speech variety of Northern Pumi, Ninglang, Yunnan province.

Notesː  Nasalized vowels are written with –ŋ instead of a diacritic over the vowel. Phonemic tone is represented with diacritics: á for high, ǎ for rising, â for falling. And that the tonal pattern of 60, 70, 80 / 600, 700, 800 / 6000, 7000, 8000 is different from the others. For discussion of tone with numerals, please refer to my dissertation: Daudey 2014:96-101.
