Other comments: Idi has a base-six counting system. Zinbi [ ɟ͡ʝibi] means ‘one only'. This base-six system is connected with the counting of yams. In traditional yam harvesting celebrations, yams are collected in piles of six and counted in this way. This is written in the orthography created for Idi in July 2011. There is another system for counting in Idi – using body parts to count. This system is also applicable to counting of yams. This system is as follows, where damana means ‘the other side’. Also, Idi people using a body parts counting system up to 19 as belowː
