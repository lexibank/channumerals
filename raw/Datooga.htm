Other comments: Asimjeeg Datooga is a variety of the Southern Nilotic dialect cluster referred to as Datooga ([tcc]) and is spoken in four communities in northern Tanzania.

 Asimjeeg Datooga has a vigesimal system.

Other comments: Datooga is spoken in Mbulu District, Tanzania. Note that 22 (ɛ́ɛ is+ATR form of áa).
Characters differing from the IPA are as follows:

<a> = [ɑ], <sh>= [ʃ], <ny> = [ɲ], <ng’> = [ŋ], <j> = [ɟ]

Word-final vowels before a pause are typically devoiced and hence tone is not transcribed on most of these vowels.
