Other comments: Jirel has a traditional vigesimal system, however, they might use Nepali numerals after twenty more frequently now. Ethnologue reports Jirel has Some comprehension of Lhasa Tibetan and some Tibetan dialects. Grammatically similar to Sherpa. Jirel is a tonal language has four contrastive tone patterns.

TONE

Jirel has four contrastive tone patterns.  The main domains of tone is the phonological word.   This word may consist of one or more syllables or one or more morphemes.  It is the realm of a correlation between pitch and contour.  In the basic tone pattern, the characteristic pitch level is confined to the initial syllable of the word, while the contour spreads over the whole word.  In monosyllabic words the contour is realized as a falling or non-falling glide.  In di- and poly-syllabic words the non-falling contour is either level or rising.  The other contour is falling.

            The contrastive tone patterns are marked with the numbers 1, 2, 3, 4 in superscript at the end of the word in the Roman transcription of Jirel.  The two high tones carry numbers 1 and 3, the two low tones the numbers 2 and 4 respectively.

            Word-initial voiced stops and affricates  / b /, / d /, / ḑ /, / j /, / g / occur only with low tone.  All other consonants can occur with either high or low tone.
