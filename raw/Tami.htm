Other comments: Tami has a quinary counting system based on hand 'lima' and man ''taumoandeʔ''. Note that all sounds represented are consistent with IPA standards with the exception of [ô] which is similar to [u] but higher up and further back. And just a brief bit of explanation on the 'numbers' larger than 100. After 100 they typically do not specify (or even try to) a specific quantity. The phrase ''akanoŋ tau tau isan'' means "plenty" or ''lots''. Adding the modifiers "tau tau" conveys then a sense of ''really a lots'' and then ''isan'' would function as a superlative conveying a sense of "too many to count".
