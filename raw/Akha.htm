Other comments: Akha has a decimal system similar to that of Hani. The creaky voice is lost on numerals 1 and 2 when it is used with classifiers tì ɣà ‘one person’, nyì ɣà ‘two people’. The tone on numeral 3 changes from low to high when used in the ten base system, sm̀ tsé ‘thirty’ not *sḿ tsé. Also, the tone on 3 is high when it precedes a classifier with a low tone, but low then the following classifier has a mid or high tone.

Akha is spoken in Burma, China, Lao, Thailand, and Vietnam, and terms for numerals such as 1,000, 10,000, 100,000 etc. have been borrowed from the respective dominant languages in these communities. However, there is an additional term tã̀ found in religious texts. The exact amount of this number is not agreed on; some say it is 1,000, others 10,000, 100,000, 1,000,000 or 10,000,000. In sum, this number and those above 999 are not understood well.
