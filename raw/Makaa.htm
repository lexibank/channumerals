Other comments: Makaa has a decimal systems, Makaa number 'one' is ŋɡúd, taking no agreement for class; it sometimes becomes ŋɡúdú when it is utterance final. The numbers sə̀màn and zàŋba are borrowed from the Ewondo language. The true Makaa numbers cwôw and tə́wèli are not used much. Thousand and hundred are English loanwords.  Makaa has three tones; á = high, à = low and â high-low or falling.
