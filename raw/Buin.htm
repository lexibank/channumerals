Other comments: The morpheme tuo occurring in Telei Buin numbers from 7 to 9 as well as in 70, 80 and 90 means “less “so the pattern is here a subtractive one. The morpheme luːɣo means “on top of” but another pattern could occur in some dialects, for example 11 is given as noikei kiikoko by Laycock (1 towards 20). The current language informants could not think of how to say 80 or 90. These two numbers were taken from Lean G.A., 2002: Counting systems of Papua New Guinea volume 3: North Solomons Province (Goroka; Papua New Guinea; Glen Lean Ethnomatheamtics Center).
