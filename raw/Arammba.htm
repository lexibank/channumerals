Other comments: The Arammba people has a six-base counting system belongs to counting and dividing yams. Each year after harvest they are put in yam houses for storage.

This counting system is still meaningful also amoung young people that live in the village: apparently they know exactly how many yams are stored in their yam house.

You can count up to 279.936 in Arammba.

1 - ngámbi

2 – yànbaru

3 - yenówe

4 - asàr

5 - tambnoy

6 - nimbo

1 group of six is called tàxwo

6 x 6 – feté

6 x 36(216) – tarumba

6 x tarumba(1296) –ndamno

6 x ndamno(7.776) – wermeke

6 x wermeke(46.656) – wi

6 x wi is called xàyo xusi  this means bad smell (too many to eat so they rot?AB)

After this they stop counting.

Example:

5000 is in Arammba: 3(yenówe) ndamno + 5

(tambnoy) tarumba + 5(tambnoy) taxwo + 2(yànmbaro)
