Other Features 

Nasalization (feature of entire morpheme), exercises influence from right to left.  The
vowel /ɑ/ has a raised allophone [ɜ] when nasalized.

Word-final layrngeals.  A word can end in /ʔ/, /h/, an abstract laryngeal (written # in
the chart above), or no laryngeal.  Words that end in the abstract laryngeal have extra-short
vowels with a ballistic quality, and the vowels /e/ and /o/ have more open allophones [ɛ]
and [ɔ].  In the transcription, a single vowel is written. Words that end in no laryngeal have
longer vowels. 

Tone levels from 1 (low) to 5 (high), including a number of phonetic and phonemic glides;
the phonemic patterns are

            1

            2

            3

            4 (realized on long vowels as 34)

            5 (realized as 35)

            13

            31

            32

In that neither the IPA accents, nor the vertical bar notation allows for the complexity of glides
on single vowels, tones are written with superscript numbers in Times New Roman.
