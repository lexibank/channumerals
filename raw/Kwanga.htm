Other comments: Kwanga counting system has three basic numeralsː 1, 2, 3 and 4 has a 'a + a' construction, however it does not contain the numeral 2 'fisi'. The number word for 5 contains a 'hand' morpheme 'tapa' which suggests that the system is a digit-tally one The constructions of 6, 7, 8, 9 and 10 are respectively: '5 + 1', '5 + 2', '5 + 3', '5 + 4', '5 + 5'. Both 15 to 20 containing a 'leg' morpheme 'sekaʔaŋi'. one man is 'himambwa'. Theoretically, in Kwanga language, you can count up to 400 (20 x 20), where there are 20 men with 40 hands and 40 legs). Beyond the number 400, they say, 'there are so many.' or they may use the western counting system.
