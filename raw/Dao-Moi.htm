Other comments: Moi counting system is very similar to that of the Maniwo dialect to the east of the Auye language, for which the number 7 is ''da:da:''. Please don't confused with the Moi language of the West Bird's Head language family.

Note that there are a couple of ways to say the numbers after twenty. The most accurate (least confusing) way is to say it is:

   1)  mée oto ku.maa.na     mée na ka  wia
        man one DEM:pl.COM.SEQ  man one OBL two

   ‘After completing one man, two on the next man. (i.e. Twenty two)’

   2)  mée oto ku.maa.na mée na ka bado ka oto
        man one DEM:pl.COM.SEQ man one OBL foot OBL one

   ‘After completing one man, one on the foot of the next man. (i.e. thirty one)’

    The phrase ‘kaas kuman’ or ‘mee oto kuman’ is dropped only when the listener is

    well aware that that the speaker is talking in the tens or twenties.  Thus when

    counting it is not necessary to say the complete phrase.  However, in order to

    avoid confusion in a normal speech, the complete phrase is obligatory.

   The highest number a Moi can say is 400 – mee ndo mee oto. (twenty twenties).
