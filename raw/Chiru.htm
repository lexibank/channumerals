The Chiru numerals from eleven to nineteen are formed by a connective /-lêj-/ which is added to sòm “ten” to the basic numerals from one to nine. Other comments: Chiru numeral system is similar to that of Mizo and other Chin languages. The numerals from eleven to nineteen are formed by a connective /-ləj-/ which is added to sòm “ten” to the basic numerals from one to nine. The numerals from twenty-one to twenty nine are formed by the same connective /-ləj-/ which is added between sòmdi “twenty” and the basic numerals from one to nine. Here /ə-/ of əkʰə̀t, ə́di and ətʰùm are also deleted. In Chiru /rəjə/ means hundred, /bɔp/ means thousand, /bɔprəjə/ means lakh and /bajbe/ means crore.
