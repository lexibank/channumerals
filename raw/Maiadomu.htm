Other comments: Maiadomu has a traditional quinary counting system up to twenty. However, nowadays, the people only keep the traditional numbers from 1 to 5. After 5, they used English numbers with phonological adaptation. Note that the traditional number for five 'dimanina' means 'one hand' but for ten is 'yauou tamoqana' means 'one me' literally, which is interesting because I wouldn’t have thought that would have appeared until 20—using both hands and both feet—but that is what they gave me.
