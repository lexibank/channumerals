Other comments: Ottawa or Oddaawaa is spoken in Central and Eastern Great Lakes, mostly on the Canadian side Southwestern Ojibwe - southern Saskatchewan and northern North Dakota, east to Thunder Bay and Michigan Upper Peninsula, including southern Manitoba and most of Minnesota. Ottawa has a base-5 system. The words from six through eight have a suffix 'waaswi' The numbers from 11 to 19 are composed of the number 'ten' plus 'aʃi' plus one of the numbers from 1-9. The numbers from 20-90 are ordinal numbers plus a suffix 'tɐ'nɐ'. Other higher numbers are as belowː
