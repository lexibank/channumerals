Other comments: Ukaan has a vigesimal numeral system with a special construction for compound numbers 15 to 19, which is formed by 20 - 5 to 1, respectively. The traditional highest number is 400 'ʊ̀hʲɔ̀nì'. Note that the [p] as in number 10 is replaced by [f] as in 11 [áfūmànûʃì], which means 'ten and one'. The 'àɡ͡bá' in 60 and other higher numbers stands for 'sack', with one 'sack' representing 'twenty'.
