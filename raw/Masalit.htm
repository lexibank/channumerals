Other comments: Masalit (spoken in Sudan) has a decimal system. The numbers 1000 and 2000 are not used in the language. For the speakers these are considered as big numbers so they use quantifiers to refer them.

Other comments: Masalit has a decimal system of counting. There are different ways to say the numbers from 11-19 and also the higher numbers. 11. tûtuk dole tu, literally means '10 above other''. This is written in orthography, not phonetically. The symbol <  ̂>on the first vowel of a word indicates +ATR for the whole word.
