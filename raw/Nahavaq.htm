Other comments: Nahavaq or Sinesip is an endangered language spoken on villages in Southern Malekula, Vanuatu. This language has a traditional quinary counting system. Now Numbers up to 10 are still in common use, but numbers over 10 are more often expressed with Bislama numerals. I was not able to find any speakers who were sure of the traditional Nahavaq numeral system beyond 200.

 

Vowels = /i/, /e/, /a/, /o/, /u/
