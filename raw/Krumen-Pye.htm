Other comments: Pye (Piè) has a quinary-vigesimal system with a simple word for fifteen, this is different from that of Tepo Krumen. This Kru language spoken in the area between Gd-Béréby and San Pedro in the Southwest of the Ivory Coast. Note that the 1 to 5, 10, 15 and 20 are basic numerals, and the 'ja' in six to nine means 'apporter / bring'. There are missing terms for 50, 60, 70, 80, 90, 1000 and 2000. Traditional numerals for 100 are 'five of twenties', 200 is or 'ten of twenties'. In orthographical spellings 1 to 10, 11, 40, 100 areː -do, 'hʋɛn, ta, ‑hɛn, hun, ‑pu tu 'o ta, ‑wlɩ 'hʋɛn, ‑hɔsrʋwɔn. Tone marks: [v́] = high tone, [v̀] = low tone,  [v̌] = Low-high tone.
