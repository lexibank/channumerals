Other comments: Juray is spoken at Gajapati district in Odisha, India, Juray still has Munda numerals from 1 to 7, Oriya numerals were borrowed from eight onwards.

Other high numbers: 400. uɲɟisɔː, 800. aʈəsɔ. Note that 'c' is the palatal plosive mostly found in many Indian languages, which is distinctive from the affricates such as in English word /teacher/.
