Other comments: The above data were take from Havasupai dialect, which has a decimal system with special substructure for numbers 6, 7 and 8. The verb phrase modifier / čˀal / means ' to come out over'. The northern Upland dialect speaker realize 20 as '10 two's', while Southern dialect (Yavapai) say  '2 ten's ' instead. In fact, the Northern Pai Indian have little opportunity to use larger figure although the above table can count up to 2,000.
