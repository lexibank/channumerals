Other comments: Sereer-Sine has a quinary, decimal system. This data was taken from variety of Sine. Serer-Sine is toneless language. The phonetic symbols ː [ɓ] and [ɗ] are implosive; [x] is a palatal fricative and [q] is an uvular stop. The data presented in Makela seems quiet similar to the data presented in Faye (1979). It must be the same variety. qarɓeen is a contraction for qarɓay –in (I don't know yet what this suffix could mean) qarɓay seems to be the plural of xarɓay which probably evolved to xarbaxay

indeed plural in sereer can be marked by an initial consonant mutation
