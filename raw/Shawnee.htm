Other comments: Shawnee has a decimal system. However, the formations for 50, 60, 70, 80, and 90 are different from those of other tensː 20, 30 and 40. This is a famous irregularity in the historical development of the higher numbers in Shawnee that linguists have known about for some time.

Shawnee has four short vowels, /i e a o/ (where /i/ and /e/ are phonetically [ɪ] and [ɛ]) and four long vowels, /iː eː aː oː/. Note that a raised dot marks vowel length.
