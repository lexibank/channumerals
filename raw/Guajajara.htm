Other comments: Quite similar to the other Tupi languages, Guajajára has a very simple counting system only have ''1, 2, 3, 4 and many'' . In recent years they have adapted the number system of Brazilian Portuguese speakers around them. In the

70s I heard of an attempt to use fingers, hands, etc., but only one mane ever tried

it and could never decide whether 20 was four hands or two hands and two feet.
