Other comments: Bura is an endangered language spoken in the southern part of

the present-day Borno State, Nigeria. Bura has a decimal system of counting. The Bura numerals listed above reveal that to form tens the cardinal units are suffixed to the morpheme –kà “ten” and –súr , from twenty up to thirty the cardinal units are suffixed to the morpheme – kùmàrì  kà “twenty” while for forty up to ninety the cardinal units are prefixed to the morpheme kùmàri; and from hundred up to a nine hundred the cardinal units are suffixed to the morpheme àrù. From one thousand up to a nine hundred the cardinal units are suffixed to the morpheme dùbù  and from one million and above the cardinal units are suffixed to the morpheme mìlìyàn. These two forms that is “dùbù and mìlìyàn” are believed to have been borrowed from the Hausa language. In the same vein, we observed that in the counting system of Bura all the cardinals, with the exception of twenty “súr” and the form for one “pál” which have high tones, all the other cardinal constituents of the language have low tones.
