Other comments: Matal: Central Chadic (ALCAM 141), spoken by several tens of thousands of speakers in the Mandara Mountains southwest of Mora, Mayo-Sava, Far North of Cameroon. Note that there are two forms for the number 'one'; dì is used in counting only ('one, two, three...'); and tēkùlā for 'one', 'a single'. 23- 29 can be deduced from the compound numerals 13 to 19. 11 /kʷlu ɡl aŋha tekʷla/ means 'ten head its one.

Notes on toneː

1. tone marks are ten tentativeː v̀ for Low, V̄ for Middle; V́ for High; and V̂ for Falling.

2. In normal (= quick) speech, the nasal (and frequently the glottal too ) of /āŋhà/ its

    is most often elided, resulting in [aː].
