Other comments: Wutung has a decimal system of counting but with what appears to be a relic of a  base-4 system, however it is odd as 6 appears to be (morphologically)  4+1, 7 is 4+2, 8 is 4+3 and 9 is 4+4. The word for 4 however is very similar to the word for 'hand' ('no' or 'noqe') and it would seem reasonable to have expected it to be quinary. Wutung speakers don't seem to have words for 100 and although they do have ways of expressing numbers up to 99 they normally use Tok Pisin for anything above the twenties. Wutung is tonal and has a quite complex 'word-tone' system where the tonal contour operates across the whole word (as opposed to the  syllable).
