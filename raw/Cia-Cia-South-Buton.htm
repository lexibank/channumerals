Wasambua is spoken by the older members of about eighty households in the neighborhood (kelingkungan) of the same name in the village of Lakambau in the southwestern quadrant of Buton Island, Southeast Sulawesi, Indonesia (5° 36.28' S 122° 35.78' E). It is unclear from the recording whether ompulu ‘ten’ is preceded by a glottal stop or not.

Note that the form satu ‘one,’ borrowed from Indonesian, is used as the initial numeral in straight counting. The form ameʔea is used in counting one of something, and in higher numeral combinations (eleven, twenty-one, etc.).
