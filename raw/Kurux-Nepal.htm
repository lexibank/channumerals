Other comments: Uraun or Nepal Kurukh has used Nepali numeral system now.

Prof. Madhav Pokharel reported (2013) checked Ferd Hahn's Kurukh grammar (1985, p. 85) and found that in Kurux the numerals inflect for gender (M, F, N). They have retained only 4 numerals (cardinals): ort-os 'one', irb-ar 'two', nub-ar 'three' and naib-ar 'four'. 

It is different to Indian Kurukh numeral system. It is related to Nepali numeral system. So its number is in Kurukh phonetics sounds, these sounds are differ from Indian or Bangladeshi Kurukh / oraon.

Note that the traditional symbol 'c' = IPA [tʃ], 'ch' = IPA [tʃʰ], 'j' = IPA [dʒ].
