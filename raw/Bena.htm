Other comments: Bena or KiBena has a decimal system.

  • Note that the affricate written orthographically as <dz> exhibits considerable dialectal variation and may be pronounced as [ts], [cç], or [tʃ]. It is generally voiceless, but some speakers use slight voicing.
  • Likewise orthographic <ky> exhibits dialectal variation (especially intervocallically), between [tʃ], [tʃj], [cçj], [cj], and [ʃj]
  • The prenasalized affricate <ndz> can also be voiceless [ⁿs]
  • There is some variation in which noun class prefix speakers will use when counting. This likely accounts for why your old data would use, for example, “lichumi” instead of my data, which is “nyikyumi”
