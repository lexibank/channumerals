提供资料的语言学家: Ms. Sandra Hufnagel and Mr. Andreas Joswig. 2011 年 5 月 9 日.

 

Other comments: Majang has a counting system based on twenty with a five-based substructure. rùùmɛr íɗítǃ literally means 'complete person', pɔ́lpɔ́lɛ́ǃ means 'fingers'. ɟóóǃ goes back to ɟóópǃ, the plural of íɗítǃ 'person'. The variation for 100 and 200 (all the numbers in between) is dialectal. The numbers 100 'ɗíbɛ́ǃ' and 1000 'kúmɛ́ǃ' are borrowing from one of nearby Cushitic language.

Other comments: Majang has a courting system based on twenty with a five-based substructure. The word 'ruumerˈɗiitʰ' in twenty means 'one complete person', that is all of his fingers and toes. 'forty' can be broken down to 'ˈpʰɔlpʰɔlɛ' means' finger/ toe, -e plural, 'd͡ʒoo' 'people', and pʰɛɛj 'two'. It is the fingers and toes of two peoples. The numbers 100 'ɗibɛ' and 1000 'kʰumɛ' are borrowing from one of nearby Cushitic language.
