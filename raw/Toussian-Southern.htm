Other comments: Southern Toussian has a quinary-vigesimal system. The number 30 'yõɡbãm' would be like 3 x 10, 'ɡbãm' means 10 but the 'yo~' has very little relation to 3 'tɔ̃́nɔ́'. For number 40 'tikininí' it might be the plural form of 20 'túkúrí' and 80 'tikiyẽ̌h' would be 20 x 4. For 100 'lèkwɛ' looks like it could be 'kwɛ 'as kwlo and lè as ... ? 20 ? It breaks the pattern. But then 200 'lèɡbãm' is 10 (ɡbãm) 20's and 1000 'kyɛɡbãm ' is 10 x 100 ? But, how did lèɡbãm become kyɛʔ and 2000 become 'kèyo'. The etymologies for these few words are still unknown.
