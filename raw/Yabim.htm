Linguist providing data and dateː Dr. Joel Bradshaw, Department of Linguistics, University of Hawaii at Manoa, Hawaii, USA. September 21, 2011.

Referenceː Otto Dempwolff, 1939, Grammar of the Jabem language in New Guinea.

Translated by Joel Bradshaw and Francisc Czobor, 2005, University of Hawaii.

提供资料的语言学家: Dr. Joel Bradshaw, 2011 年 9 月 21 日.

 

Other comments: Yabem has a quinary system with one word for twenty 'ŋaʔ samuʔ (tɛŋ)' which mean 'person whole (one)'. Generally only numbers 1 - 5 and 10 are regularly used now. They usually use English numbers. Like Bukawac, Yabem is a tonal Austronesian language, too.
